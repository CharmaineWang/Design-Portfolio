Understanding Design Systems

Since this was my first time help build a design system, I chatted with Brad our product design lead (also my mentor) and did some research to wrap my head around design systems.

I started with a quick read of the Design System Handbook by DesignBetter and learned that having a scalable design system not only allows teams to design consistently, it also allows for faster prototyping and iterations, improve usability, allow the team to scale and build accessible products. These findings really helped me appreciate the impact a well-structured design system can make on teams and helped me understand how it can help solve our problems at RBC.

I‚Äôve used design systems such as Material Design and Apple Human Interface Guidelines in the past as a guide when designing for Android and iOS products. While they are indeed powerful and informative, the Mobile team was looking for something that was a bit more detailed. While coming up with a solution for our own design system, I referenced Shopify Polaris and Atlassian Design System as both seem to have the level of detail we were looking for at RBC.

The hypothesis was that using words such as ‚Äúenum‚Äù, ‚Äúboolean‚Äù, ‚Äútrue‚Äù and ‚Äúfalse‚Äù would make it easier for developers to understand how to build certain components.

Self and Team Testing

Due to the nature of an internal tool, the best way to test this product was for our team members to interact with the design system and talk through their experiences. This included trying to design screens using documented components and document new components.

While I tried it myself and asked other people to use it, I made sure to look out for several things. For the purpose of using the design system, I thought about:
- How was the component used
- Were the constraints flexible and easily understood
- Was the component documentation easy to follow

And to understand how approachable the documentation style is, I observed the following:
- How long did it take to document a new component
- Were they able to document everything they needed
- Where did the designer get stuck on

Observations

After a few rounds of trying to use the design system and documenting new components, we quickly realized that most designers on the team don‚Äôt have a coding background and so this language of variables and data type made it difficult to understand and document. We‚Äôd basically have to teach everyone on the team how to code before onboarding everyone to this framework.

Another realization I had was although the Layout section provided different scenarios components usage along with its constraints, it still lacked detail in terms of how each part of a component would respond to screen sizes.

As a member of the team, I wanted to ensure that the documentation style is easily replicable and understood so that current designers can understand and document components at a faster rate while future hires can easily onboard.

Result

After a few rounds of iterating, I landed with a documentation style that was straight forward and can be applied to any component.

Here are the key features of the final design:
1) Each component is broken down into smaller parts that are labelled to help distinguish them.
2) I showcased how to build the component using each part by using examples showing how the parts interact with one another
3) I decided to forgo the techy terms and simply use words and sentences to explain the constraints.

Learnings and Reflections

One of the big takeaways I gained from this experience was the importance of having a functional design system. Design systems not only define quality and consistency across a product, but it also provides designers with the confidence and time to focus on solving user problems by abstracting away many ambiguities. Another thing I learned is that designers solve problems, which can range from consumer facing products to internal tools. This was my first time designing for an internal use case which meant there was a smaller user group. However, it was important to keep in mind of future users and design for scalability.

Looking back, I‚Äôd love to involve PMs, accessibility experts, content writers and more developers throughout the process. I think it would‚Äôve been beneficial for everybody aside from solely designers to understand the value and importance of having a design system.
